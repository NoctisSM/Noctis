Competitive analysis

I opened and then analyzed these three websites, one of which is a direct competitor and two are indirect. I compared the ordering experience of each website as a new user and a returning user. What's more is I juxtaposed negative comments from the App Store and Android App Store to get the essence of what the users struggle with the most while using them and what they are not too keen on.

Bloomingdale's

Website Screenshot Placeholder

The Good

  • The website has simple, modern and clean design with big headings and colors with high contrast.
  • The meaning behind the UI icons is explained.
  • The ordering process is smooth and there are many filters and categories of products.

The Bad

  • The navigation is unintuitive and takes time to get used to.
  • The expand feature seems sometimes too long. Thus, it should be folded on default.
  • Names of some products are clipped.

Asos

Website Screenshot Placeholder

The Good

  • The navigation icons are big and have labels under them.
  • There are filters for different clothing types, such as men, women, and children.
  • The website is available in different languages.

The Bad

  • Light gray text on the white background has a contrast ratio of 1.77, thus it's not accessible.
  • There is no possibility to create an account using social links.

Nike

Website Screenshot Placeholder

The Good

  • Modern and clean design with big headings and colors with high contrast.
  • Customizable products, variety of products to choose from.
  • Clear brand identity, including colors, logo, typeface, style, motion, imagery, and photography.

The Bad

  • Icons sometimes don't have labels underneath.
  • The menu makes it a little bit hard to navigate.
