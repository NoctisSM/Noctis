Competitive analysis

I opened and then analyzed these three websites, one of which is a direct competitor, and two are indirect. I compared the ordering experience of each website as a new user and a returning user. What's more is I juxtaposed negative comments from the App Store and Android App Store to get the essence of what the users struggle with the most while using them and what they are not too keen on.

Getting feedback

  • After the lo-fi wireframes were designed, I made a prototype to test on a couple of users.
  • I made a research plan with goals, KPIs, methodology, and participant info.
  • After collecting insights, I made an affinity diagram to seek patterns in behavior and frustrations.
  • Usability study presentation showcased quick recap, insights, and recommendations.

Prototype validation

After the prototype was created, I tested it on 5 users. I made a research plan outlining research goals, questions, KPIs, methodology, info about participants, and a script with tasks. The main focus was ensuring a smooth ordering process without friction, leading to product purchase and pickup. Tested and carried out with Figma’s prototype mirror share app.

Tasks included adding items to the cart, multiplying quantities, checking out, using coupon codes, and toggling description tabs. Participants completed a System Usability Scale questionnaire. After collecting insights, I made an affinity diagram to organize ideas.

The usability study presentation then showcased the insights and recommendations.

Study results

60% of participants (3 out of 5) weren’t able to purchase any product(s). However, all users could add desired items to their carts, use coupon codes, and toggle the description button. The main friction: unaware that payment required a credit card. PayPal and Stripe were later added to satisfy customer frustration.
