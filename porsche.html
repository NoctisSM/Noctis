Project Overview

Porsche's online car configurator offered extensive customization options with hundreds of possible combinations for exterior colors, interior materials, wheels, and packages. While this flexibility was a strength, research revealed that many users felt overwhelmed by the sheer number of choices, leading to analysis paralysis and abandoned configurations.

As the lead designer on this project, I was tasked with creating a new feature called Design Recommendations that would help users discover expertly curated configurations as a starting point for their customization journey. The goal was to reduce decision fatigue while maintaining the premium brand experience and preserving the freedom of full customization.

The Challenge

Through stakeholder interviews and preliminary user research, we identified several critical issues affecting the configuration experience.

  • Users spent an average of 47 minutes configuring their vehicle, but 64% abandoned without saving or sharing their configuration
  • Overwhelming number of options created anxiety about making the wrong choices or missing optimal combinations
  • Lack of guidance on which colors, materials, and packages complemented each other aesthetically
  • Users wanted inspiration and expert recommendations without losing the ability to fully customize
  • Difficulty visualizing how different options would look together before committing to selections
  • No clear starting point led to aimless clicking through options without a clear direction
How might we provide curated expert recommendations that inspire confidence and accelerate decision-making while preserving the flexibility and personalization that define the Porsche brand experience?

User Research

To understand the needs and frustrations of Porsche customers during the configuration process, I conducted in-depth research, including surveys, interviews, and observation sessions at Porsche Experience Centers, where customers configure vehicles with product specialists.

Research Methods

  • Online survey with 156 respondents who had used the Porsche configurator in the past year
  • One-on-one interviews with 18 participants, including recent buyers and prospective customers
  • Observational sessions at three Porsche Experience Centers, watching 12 configuration appointments
  • Analysis of configurator analytics, including drop-off points and completion rates
  • Review of customer service inquiries related to configuration questions

KEY INSIGHT FROM RESEARCH

Users do not want to be told what to choose, but they desperately want guidance and validation. Expert recommendations should serve as an inspiring starting point that demonstrates what is possible while preserving complete freedom to customize. The key is framing recommendations as design inspiration rather than limitations.

Design Solution

The Design Recommendations feature introduces a curated gallery of expert-selected configurations at the beginning of the configuration journey. Each recommendation is thoughtfully composed by Porsche design experts and presented with a clear rationale for why certain elements work together harmoniously.

Core Principles

  • Recommendations as inspiration rather than prescription
  • Clear design rationale builds understanding and confidence
  • Seamless transition from recommendation to full customization
  • Premium presentation reflecting Porsche brand values
  • Preserve complete freedom while reducing initial paralysis
[Design Recommendations gallery showing three curated builds]

Key Features

Curated Design Cards

Each recommendation is presented as a premium card featuring high-quality 3D renders with immersive color-matched backgrounds. The cards include clear naming conventions that evoke the character of each build, such as Shade Green Metallic with SportDesign Package or Gentian Blue Metallic with Slate Grey and Chalk Leather Interior.

[Curated design card with vehicle render and details]

Detailed View Pages

Clicking into any recommendation reveals a comprehensive detail page showcasing multiple angles, interior views, and close-ups of materials and finishes. Each page includes a complete breakdown of all selected options with explanations of how they complement each other.

[Detail page showing multiple vehicle angles and specifications]

Dual Call to Action Strategy

Every recommendation offers two clear paths forward. Users can either start with this design to jump directly into the configurator with all options pre selected, or explore details first to learn more before committing. This accommodates both decisive users and those who need more information.

Design Philosophy Context

Each recommendation includes expert commentary explaining the design thinking behind the configuration. This educational component helps users understand principles of automotive design and builds confidence in making their own choices.

Information Architecture

I restructured the configurator entry experience to introduce Design Recommendations as an optional but prominent starting point. The new flow preserves the ability to configure from scratch while making recommendations immediately accessible.

  • Smooth transitions and micro interactions

Prototyping and Testing

I created interactive prototypes to test the Design Recommendations feature with users before development. Testing was conducted in two phases with a total of 24 participants, including both Porsche owners and prospective customers.

Testing Methodology

Participants were given scenario-based tasks such as finding a configuration that matched their style preferences, understanding what made certain combinations work well together, and moving from a recommendation into full customization mode. I used a combination of moderated in person sessions at Porsche Experience Centers and remote unmoderated testing.

Phase One Findings

  • 92% of participants engaged with Design Recommendations before attempting to configure from scratch
  • Users appreciated the immersive color-matched backgrounds, calling them premium and showroom-like
  • The Curated Build badge successfully communicated expert selection and increased trust
  • Some users wanted more recommendations to choose from beyond the initial three
  • Detail pages needed a clearer distinction between what was included versus optional additions

Design Iterations

Based on testing feedback, I expanded the recommendations gallery from three to six curated builds covering a broader range of styles from sporty to elegant to timeless. I refined the detail pages to clearly separate standard features, included options, and suggested additions. The Start with this design call to action was made more prominent with clearer messaging about what would happen next.

Phase Two Results

  • Configuration completion rates improved from 36% to 78% when users started with recommendations
  • Average time to complete configuration decreased from 47 minutes to 22 minutes
  • User confidence scores increased by 54% when asked about satisfaction with their final choices
  • 89% of participants said they would use Design Recommendations in a real purchase scenario

PROJECT SUMMARY AND REFLECTION

This eight-month internship project, redesigning the entry experience for the Porsche configurator, taught me valuable lessons about balancing guidance with freedom in user experience design. The challenge was creating a feature that reduced decision fatigue without making users feel their choices were being made for them.

One of the most important insights came early in the research phase when I observed configuration sessions at Porsche Experience Centers. Watching product specialists guide customers through options, I noticed they never told customers what to choose. Instead, they showed inspiring examples and explained design principles, empowering customers to make confident decisions. This observation became the foundation for how we approached Design Recommendations.

The competitive analysis revealed that while other luxury brands offered preset configurations, these often felt generic or purely functional rather than aspirational. Porsche had an opportunity to differentiate by emphasizing design philosophy and aesthetic harmony. The decision to include expert rationale for each recommendation transformed them from simple presets into educational tools that built user confidence.

The immersive color-matched backgrounds on recommendation cards emerged as one of the most impactful visual design decisions. During testing, multiple participants commented that these backgrounds made the vehicles feel more tangible and aspirational compared to generic studio environments. This detail elevated the premium feel of the experience and helped users envision themselves with that particular configuration.

Usability testing provided critical validation and revealed areas for refinement. The dramatic improvement in completion rates from 36% to 78% and reduction in configuration time from 47 to 22 minutes demonstrated that recommendations successfully reduced decision paralysis. However, equally important was the 54% increase in confidence scores, showing that users felt better about their choices rather than simply making faster decisions.

This project reinforced several principles that guide my approach to design. First, reducing options is not always the answer to complexity. Sometimes users need better guidance through complexity rather than artificial simplification. Second, education and transparency build trust more effectively than hiding information. Third, premium experiences come from attention to detail and craft, not just from visual polish. The Design Recommendations feature successfully achieved its goal of providing an inspiring starting point that accelerated configuration while preserving the freedom and personalization central to the Porsche brand.
