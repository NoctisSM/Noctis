Project Overview

I redesigned the entry experience for Porsche's online car configurator to reduce decision paralysis and improve completion rates for customers configuring luxury vehicles.

Porsche's configurator offered hundreds of customization options across exterior colors, interior materials, wheels, and performance packages. While this flexibility was a key selling point, 64% of users abandoned their configuration without completing it, spending an average of 47 minutes in analysis paralysis. I worked with Porsche's digital team to create a Design Recommendations feature that provided expert guidance while preserving the freedom customers expected from a luxury brand.

Role & Context

I served as product designer on a small team working directly with Porsche's configurator product lead and engineering stakeholders. My role focused on user research, interaction design, and visual design for the recommendations experience. Working at Porsche taught me to balance prescriptive guidance with creative freedom, a tension unique to luxury e-commerce where customers expect both expertise and autonomy. I learned that premium customers don't want to be told what to choose, but desperately need validation that their choices work together aesthetically. This insight, gained through observational research at Porsche Experience Centers, became the foundation for how we framed recommendations as inspiration rather than limitation.

Key Initiatives

Conducted research with 156 survey respondents, 18 in-depth interviews, and 12 observational sessions at Porsche Experience Centers to understand configuration behavior and pain points.

Designed a curated recommendations gallery featuring expert-selected configurations with immersive color-matched backgrounds and educational rationale for each design choice.

Created seamless transitions from recommendation selection to full customization, ensuring users could start with inspiration but maintain complete control over their final build.

The Problem

Too much choice creates paralysis at the point of purchase

Research revealed that Porsche customers struggled with the overwhelming number of configuration options presented simultaneously. Survey data showed 71% found there were too many options to choose from, while 68% were unsure which colors and materials complemented each other aesthetically. Users spent an average of 47 minutes configuring their vehicle, yet 64% abandoned without saving their configuration or contacting a dealer.

The root issue wasn't the number of options but the lack of a clear starting point. Customers wanted expert guidance on what looked good together, but feared losing the creative control that justified the premium price. Competitors offered generic presets, but these felt impersonal and didn't build confidence. Observing configuration sessions at Porsche Experience Centers revealed a critical insight: product specialists never prescribed specific choices. Instead, they showed inspiring examples, explained design principles, and empowered customers to make confident decisions themselves.

This created a significant business problem. High abandonment rates meant lost conversions, while lengthy configuration times indicated user frustration rather than engaged exploration. The digital configurator needed to replicate the Experience Center approach: provide expert inspiration without removing customer autonomy.

The Solution

Expert curation as design education, not prescription

The Design Recommendations feature introduced a curated gallery of expert-selected configurations at the start of the configuration journey. Each recommendation showcased a complete vehicle build with high-quality 3D visualization, immersive color-matched backgrounds that previewed the aesthetic, and clear naming that evoked the character of each design. Most importantly, each recommendation included expert commentary explaining why specific colors, materials, and packages complemented each other, transforming presets into educational tools.

Every recommendation offered two pathways forward. Users could immediately start building with a recommendation to jump directly into the full configurator with all options pre-selected, or they could explore details first to review the complete specification and understand the design thinking. This dual approach accommodated both decisive customers and those needing more information before committing. The seamless transition from recommendation to full customization ensured users never felt locked in; they simply had a better starting point than a blank canvas.

The visual design reinforced the educational intent. Custom color-matched backgrounds immersed users in each configuration's aesthetic before they committed. The layout prioritized the vehicle visualization while keeping specifications accessible, balancing inspiration with information. Users could save configurations started from recommendations alongside fully custom builds, ensuring the feature enhanced rather than replaced existing functionality.

Impact

Measurable improvements in completion and confidence

The Design Recommendations feature dramatically improved key metrics. Configuration completion rates increased from 36% to 78% when users started with a recommendation, representing a 116% improvement in conversion. Average time to complete configuration decreased from 47 minutes to 22 minutes, a 53% reduction that indicated reduced paralysis rather than rushed decisions. Post-configuration surveys showed a 54% increase in user confidence scores when asked about satisfaction with final choices. Perhaps most validating, 89% of test participants indicated they would use recommendations in a real purchase scenario, demonstrating the solution resonated with actual Porsche customers rather than just performing well in testing.
