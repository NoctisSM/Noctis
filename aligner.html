Competitive analysis

To understand Invisalign.com’s strengths and areas for growth, I compared its homepage journey, offer presentation, and digital experience with its principal consumer competitors: OrthoFX and SmileDirectClub.

About the research: I performed heuristic walkthroughs (desktop + mobile), catalogued onboarding, visual messaging, conversion triggers, and accessibility features for each platform. Shown below are key differences and sample findings.

Invisalign.com

Homepage Screenshot Placeholder

The Good

  • Compelling hero visuals communicating product confidence.
  • Clear evidence/credibility (doctor-recommended, "20 million smiles" stat).
  • The three-step treatment overview is approachable for new users.
  • Mobile-friendly, easy-to-read typography and strong call-to-action buttons.
  • Accessible color contrast on majority of elements; AA compliance in key areas.

The Bad

  • Some information-dense FAQ sections can feel overwhelming to first-time users.
  • Important calls to action (doctor search, "find a provider") are occasionally buried in the flow.
  • No virtual try-on or AR preview is available, unlike some DTC competitors.

OrthoFX

Homepage Screenshot Placeholder (OrthoFX)

The Good

  • Leading with user-friendly comparison charts for cost and treatment duration.
  • Immediate virtual consult options available from homepage.
  • Distinct color-coded plans and transparent pricing communication.

The Bad

  • Less mature visual identity, with some inconsistent spacing and interactive elements.
  • Mobile site has slightly cluttered call-to-action stack above the fold.
  • Less clinical/credentialed focus; feels more DTC and less "medically authoritative".

SmileDirectClub

Homepage Screenshot Placeholder (SmileDirectClub)

The Good

  • Direct-to-consumer sign-up is simple and fast (no office visit required).
  • Use of interactive "smile preview" tool to visualize results on photos.
  • Strong, playful visual language with lots of customer "before and after" stories.

The Bad

  • Lacks in-depth provider information and offline options compared to Invisalign.
  • Support and FAQ buried under marketing copy; less focus on the science or credentials.
  • Accessibility for visual impairment is not as robust (low contrast in hero areas, etc).

Personas

I synthesized two core personas based on user surveys and stakeholder interviews to encapsulate key needs for Invisalign.com shoppers and prospects.

"I want a treatment that fits my busy work and family schedule, and I want to see what my smile could look like before I commit."

Maria Vasquez

Age36
LocationPhoenix, AZ
OccupationTeacher
Family StatusParent

Frustrations

  • Uncertain about insurance coverage for clear aligners.
  • Overwhelmed by technical/scientific info or pop-up modals.
  • Wants to know the likely outcome before committing time/money.

Goals

  • A simple online preview of how her teeth could look post-treatment.
  • A way to connect with a local doctor or support specialist.
  • Clear, upfront pricing with no hidden fees.
About Maria

Maria is busy balancing her teaching career and young kids. Shopping for clear aligners must be on her own time and feel low-stress, with easy access to in-person care if needed. She values transparent pricing and service guarantees.
"I want treatment I can do from home, but I still want real dental supervision and fast customer support."

Jordan Lee

Age29
LocationDenver, CO
OccupationProduct Manager
Family StatusSingle

Frustrations

  • Comparison overload between DTC brands and doctors' offices.
  • Can't easily book a consult without a phone call or email tag.
  • Unclear billing or shipping policies for impression kits.

Goals

  • Self-directed treatment tracking from home.
  • Direct line to customer support and dental professional.
  • Personalized, digital-first onboarding experience.
About Jordan

Jordan expects most services to be available digitally and on demand. He wants clear aligner treatment with maximum convenience and expects transparency, easy access to real support, and a sense of control throughout the process.

Accessibility check

Accessibility Screenshot Placeholder

Conducted a full accessibility audit. Most homepage contrast ratios meet AA standards, and alt tags are present, but the interactive FAQ and hero banner have some areas for improvement. The mobile experience is strong for colorblind individuals and those with low vision, except for smaller CTA text.

User survey
