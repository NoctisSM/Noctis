Invisalign (2021)

Challenge
Even though Invisalign® offered a modern alternative to braces, many people didn’t know where to start. Between treatment options, costs, and uncertainty about how long it would take, potential patients often felt overwhelmed. The idea of straightening teeth was tied to discomfort, restrictions, and long commitments. Without a clear and approachable entry point, many simply delayed getting started despite wanting change.

Solution
During my time researching the Invisalign experience, I explored ways to make the treatment journey more intuitive and inviting. The focus was on guidance without pressure, helping people feel confident about beginning their transformation. The redesigned flow centered around three main ideas:

Personalized starting points using 3D scans to show what their new smile could look like.

Clear next steps from meeting the doctor to seeing early results in just weeks.

Flexible control, allowing patients to remove aligners, adjust to their lifestyle, and progress comfortably at their own pace.

The process was designed to balance structure with freedom, giving people the sense that they were being guided, not constrained.

Impact
The new approach gave patients a more natural and confident way to begin their smile journey. What once felt intimidating became approachable. Early feedback highlighted a few clear shifts:

Patients felt more in control and excited from the very beginning.

The experience was less clinical and more personal, removing the anxiety around orthodontic treatment.

Doctors noticed higher engagement, with patients arriving more informed and motivated.

By rethinking the start of the Invisalign experience, the process became smoother and faster, turning what used to be a complex medical decision into an empowering first step toward a new smile.
